Frequently Asked Questions
What can a BridgePoint Inheritance Advance be used for?

The funds can be used for anything at your discretion. Executors or trustees typically use an advance to cover any estate debts, bills or expenses like mortgages or maintenance costs, and it can also be used for funeral costs. An Inheritance Advance can also be used to maximize the value of the estate by updating or renovating the estate property for a higher resale price. Beneficiaries use Inheritance Advances in numerous ways including paying off bills or debt, down payments for new properties, investments or personal expenditures.

Are there restrictions on what I can use an Inheritance Advance for?

No. They can be used at your own discretion.

How much can I borrow using an Inheritance Advance?

Our inheritance loans start at $5,000 with no limit on the maximum loan amount. The approved amount will be based on a review of your file and anticipated inheritance.

What is probate and how does it affect my inheritance?

Probate is a legal process that takes place after someone passes away and includes proving in court that a deceased person's will is valid (usually a routine matter) identifying and inventorying the deceased person's property and distributing the remaining property as the will directs. Unfortunately, the probate process and the administration of the estate can take months and even years to conclude.

What do you need from me to approve my Inheritance Advance?

All we need is a completed application to get the process started. We will be in touch with you to request any additional information.

Does BridgePoint require a credit check or proof of income?

No. You application is based on the value of the estate or anticipated inheritance so a credit check and proof or income are not required.

When do I have to pay back my Inheritance Advance?

Repayment is not required until your estate has been finalized and you are in receipt of the estate funds. You can repay the entire loan or set up a payment plan at any point before the estate is finalized without any penalty or fees.

How can I use an Inheritance Advance to maximize the value of the estate?

In today’s housing market, updating or renovating the inherited property(s) can increase the resale price significantly which consequently increases the amount each beneficiary receives. An Inheritance Advance can be utilized to cover the costs up front and simply repaid when the estate is finalized and property(s0 sold.

Is there a difference between an Inheritance Advance and an Inheritance Loan?

No. An Inheritance Advance and an Inheritance Loan are the same thing, however an Advance best describes this solution because they are funds that you will eventually receive via inheritance you are entitled to.
